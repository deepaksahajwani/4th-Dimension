## 3. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

### 3.1 Mandatory PPE

| Equipment | When Required |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Hard Hat** | Always on active construction sites |
| **Safety Shoes** | Always (steel toe preferred) |
| **High-Vis Vest** | Always on sites with moving vehicles |
| **Safety Glasses** | When overhead work/debris risk |

### 3.2 Situation-Specific PPE

| Situation | Additional PPE |
|-----------|----------------|
| Concrete work | Rubber boots, gloves |
| Demolition area | Dust mask, eye protection |
| Elevated areas | Fall protection harness |
| Loud machinery | Ear protection |
| Night visits | Reflective gear, torch |

### 3.3 PPE Provided by Company

Collect from Admin before your first site visit:
- Hard hat (with 4D logo)
- High-vis vest
- Safety glasses

*Replace damaged PPE immediately*

## 4. Site Hazards & Precautions

### 4.1 Common Hazards

| Hazard | Precaution |
|--------|------------|
| **Falls from height** | Stay away from edges, use barriers, harness if required |
| **Falling objects** | Hard hat always, watch for work above |
| **Trips and falls** | Watch your step, clear pathways |
| **Moving vehicles** | Stay in designated walkways, eye contact with drivers |
| **Open excavations** | Maintain safe distance, use barriers |
| **Electrical hazards** | Don't touch exposed wires, inform contractor |
| **Structural instability** | Don't enter without clearance |
| **Hot/cold stress** | Dress appropriately, stay hydrated |

### 4.2 Restricted Areas

**Never enter without escort:**
- Active excavation zones
- Crane operation areas
- Demolition zones
- Confined spaces
- Energized electrical rooms
- Areas marked with warning tape

## 6. Photography Guidelines

### 6.1 Safe Photography

- Stop walking before taking photos
- Never lean over edges for photos
- Don't use flash in confined spaces
- Be aware of surroundings
- Don't photograph workers without consent

### 6.2 Restricted Photography

- Check with contractor about photography policies
- Some areas may have restrictions
- Sensitive information may need redaction

## 7. Emergency Procedures

### 7.1 In Case of Emergency

1. **Stop and assess** the situation
2. **Alert** those around you
3. **Call for help** (site supervisor / 112)
4. **Evacuate** if instructed
5. **Assemble** at designated point
6. **Report** to site supervisor

### 7.2 Emergency Contacts

| Contact | Number |
|---------|--------|
| Emergency Services | 112 |
| Fire | 101 |
| Ambulance | 102 |
| 4D Office | [Office Number] |
| Site Supervisor | [Get on arrival] |

### 7.3 First Aid

- Know location of first aid kit
- Don't move injured persons (unless danger)
- Call for medical help
- Provide basic first aid if trained

---

## 8. Incident Reporting

### 8.1 Report Immediately

- Any injury (even minor)
- Near-miss incidents
- Unsafe conditions observed
- Property damage
- Unauthorized activities

### 8.2 How to Report

1. Inform site supervisor immediately
2. Note details (time, location, people involved)
3. Take photos if safe
4. Report to 4D office same day
5. Complete incident report form

---

## 9. Weather Considerations

### 9.1 Hot Weather

- Wear light colored clothing
- Carry water bottle
- Take breaks in shade
- Watch for heat exhaustion signs
- Avoid peak sun hours if possible

### 9.2 Rain/Wet Conditions

- Wear waterproof footwear
- Surfaces may be slippery
- Avoid excavations
- Postpone if unsafe
- Watch for electrical hazards

### 9.3 Poor Visibility

- Don't visit sites in fog/heavy rain
- Ensure adequate lighting
- Wear reflective gear
- Avoid night visits unless essential

---

## 10. Special Considerations

### 10.1 For Women on Site

- Use designated facilities
- Travel with colleague when possible
- Inform office of visit times
- Report any harassment immediately
- Dress appropriately for site conditions

### 10.2 For Those with Health Conditions

- Inform your supervisor
- Carry required medications
- Avoid overexertion
- Stay hydrated
- Take breaks as needed
